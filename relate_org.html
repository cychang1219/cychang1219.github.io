National University Astronomy Society Alliance of Taiwan

In 2014, when university astronomy clubs were facing major difficulties in the age of the internet, several club presidents began a journey of cross-school cooperation and mutual assistance, gradually integrating diverse communities across different regions from their previously independent organizations.
On March 16, 2019, the National University Astronomy Society Alliance of the Republic of China was officially established as a national social organization. Currently, the alliance is based on more than 20 university astronomy societies, with the board of directors, supervisors, and various executive officers mainly composed of retired astronomy society operators. The alliance actively promotes the following tasks:
University astronomy community mutual assistance platform: integrating the experience, knowledge, and resources of astronomy clubs, promoting inter-school exchanges, and assisting current club operators.

National Tsing Hua University Astronomy Club

Established on November 29, 1971, the National Tsing Hua University Astronomy Club is the organizing body for the Sky Kids Astronomy Camp and the founding club of the National University Astronomy Society Alliance. They have four main telescopes with a diameter of over 10 cm, a guiding system, a Takahashi EM-200 high-precision equatorial mount, and a Canon EOS 60D astronomical modified DSLR camera.
Their mission is to promote "amateur astronomy photography," "astronomy popularization and education," and to establish and facilitate the exchange of the university astronomy community.
